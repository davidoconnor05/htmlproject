The Parts of a PC

Central Processing Unit (CPU)

  

The Central Processing Unit (CPU) is the brain of the computer.The CPU interprets, processes and executes instructions, most often from the hardware and software programs running on the device. The CPU performs arithmetic, logic, and other operations to transform data input into more usable information output.
Clock Speed:
- Measured in GHz (gigahertz).
- Represents how many cycles a CPU can execute per second.
Core Components:
- Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU): Performs mathematical and logical operations.
- Control Unit: Manages the execution of instructions.
Function:
- Executes instructions from computer programs.
- Performs arithmetic and logical operations.

RAM (Memory)

  

Random Access Memory (RAM) is temporary storage used by the computer to store data being used or processed.

  
RAM (Random Access Memory) Overview:
- Type of computer memory.
- Used for temporary storage of actively used data.
- Volatile memory (loses data when power is off).
Purpose and Function:
- Facilitates quick access to data for the CPU.
- Stores the operating system, applications, and active data.
Read/Write Operations:
- Allows both reading and writing of data.
- Supports dynamic changes during operation.

Storage

  

Storage devives are used to store data on a long term basis. examples of storage devices include, HDDs(Hard Drive Disks) and SSDs(Solid State Drives)

  
SSD: PROS: CONS:
Row1: High-Speed Performance: Price: generally more expensive the HDDS
Row2: Energy Efficiency: SSDS consume less power then HDDS Capacity Constraints: High capacity SSDs are expensive limiting how much storage you have
HDD: PROS: CONS:
Row1: Mass Storage: HDDs are cheaper then SSDs and allow alot more storage space Mechanical Parts: using mechanical parts means HDDs are more likely to experience failure
Row2: Widespread Compatability- HDDs have been around for along time and are compatible with most devices Slower Speeds: HDDs are slower than SSDs in terms of read and write speeds, which can impact overall system performance.

Motherboard

  

The Motherboard is the circuit board that connects all of your hardware to your processor, distributes electricity from your power supply, and defines the types of storage devices, memory modules, and graphics cards (among other expansion cards) that can connect to your PC.

Power Supply

  

The main function of a power supply is to convert electric current from a source to the correct voltage, current, and frequency to power the load

Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)

  

A Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) is a computer chip that renders graphics and images by performing rapid mathematical calculations. GPUs are used for both professional and personal computing.
