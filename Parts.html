The Parts of a PC

Central Processing Unit (CPU)

The Central Processing Unit (CPU) is the brain of the computer. The CPU interprets, processes and executes instructions, most often from the hardware and software programs running on the device. The CPU performs arithmetic, logic, and other operations to transform data input into more usable information output.


The clock speed of a CPU, measured in gigahertz (GHz), is a fundamental specification of a CPU, indicating the number of cycles it can execute per second. The core components of a CPU include the Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU), responsible for mathematical and logical operations, and the Control Unit, which manages instruction execution. The primary function of a CPU revolves around executing instructions from computer programs, encompassing a range of tasks, from basic arithmetic to complex logical operations. These core components work in tandem to carry out the essential functions that drive the computational power of a computer.

Random Access Memory (RAM)

RAM, or Random Access Memory, is a pivotal type of computer memory employed for the temporary storage of actively used data. Functioning as volatile memory, it retains information only when powered on and loses data when the power is off. The primary purpose of RAM is to facilitate swift access to data for the CPU, serving as a dynamic workspace for the operating system, applications, and actively processed data. Its capability for both read and write operations enables the seamless and dynamic manipulation of data during system operation, allowing for efficient and real-time responsiveness in various computing tasks.

Storage

Storage devives are used to store data on a long term basis. examples of storage devices include, HDD's (Hard Drive Disks) and SSD's (Solid State Drives)

Motherboard

The Motherboard is the circuit board that connects all of your hardware to your processor, distributes electricity from your power supply, and defines the types of storage devices, memory modules, and graphics cards (among other expansion cards) that can connect to your PC.

Power Supply

The main function of a PC power supply is to convert electrical power from an external source, typically a wall outlet, into a form that is suitable for powering the components within a computer. This includes converting alternating current (AC) from the outlet to direct current (DC) used by the computer's internal components. The power supply ensures a stable and regulated supply of power to the motherboard, CPU, graphics card, storage devices, and other hardware components. It also provides various power connectors to supply electricity to different parts of the computer, ensuring they receive the appropriate voltage and current. In essence, the power supply is a critical component that delivers the necessary electrical power to enable the proper functioning of a computer system.

Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)

A Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) is a computer chip that renders graphics and images by performing rapid mathematical calculations. GPUs are used for both professional and personal computing.
